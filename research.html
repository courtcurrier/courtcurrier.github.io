Research

My research focuses on the ecology of savannas and grasslands and how they adapt to disturbances such as fire, grazing, and climate change. The places I study are usually dry and already experience these disturbances, which define these systems! But I study the effects of novel disturbance regimes, when ecosystems are pushed beyond historical magnitude or duration of disturbance events. I am an empirical scientist, using long-term and large-scale field experiments combined with cutting-edge laboratory analyses.

Current Projects

• Fire, grazing, and aridity controls on soil carbon storage and persistence
• Bigeochemical cycles and acclimation to global change drivers
• Soil microbial responses to disturbance, and mechanistic feedbacks to C and N cycling
• Grassland resilience across aridity gradients
